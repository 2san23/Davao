Davao Crocodile Park

Davao Crocodile Park & Zoo is a world-class wildlife conservation center in Davao City that showcases a "state-of-the-art" crocodile farming system. Beyond its hundreds of crocodiles—including "Pangil," one of the largest in the country—the park operates as a mini-zoo home to tigers, orangutans, exotic birds, and raptors.

Visiting Information (as of March 2026)

  • Operating Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM daily.
  • Entrance Fees: Regular: ₱450 (3 years old and above). Discounted: ₱350 for PWDs and Senior Citizens (with valid ID). Free: Children 2 years old and below. Special Promo: Senior Citizens enter for FREE every Tuesday.
  • Daily Shows: Animal shows and "Keeper Talks" typically occur twice daily at 10:00 AM and 4:00 PM.

Key Attractions & Activities

  • Wildlife Encounters: Get close to various reptiles, monkeys, and the famous Black Palm Cockatoo named George.
  • Tribu K'Mindanawan: A cultural village within the complex that often features spectacular Fire Dance Shows (usually Friday–Sunday evenings).
  • Exotic Dining: The nearby Riverwalk Grill is famous for serving crocodile sisig, crocodile ice cream, and ostrich egg omelets.
  • Education: The park includes a crocodile incubator room where visitors can learn about the life cycle of these "living fossils".

Visitor Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: Arrive in the late afternoon to catch the 4:00 PM show and stay for the cultural performances.
  • Weather Prep: The park is mostly outdoors and can get very hot; bringing an umbrella, extra water, and a small fan is highly recommended.
  • Butterfly House: Your ticket usually includes entry to the nearby Butterfly House and Tribu K'Mindanawan.
