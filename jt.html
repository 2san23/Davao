Japanese Tunnel

The Japanese Tunnel (officially D' Japanese Tunnel Family Resort and Restaurant) is a major World War II historical site in Davao City. Dug by Filipino prisoners of war under the direction of the Japanese Imperial Army in 1942, it served as a military hideout, storage facility, and headquarters during the Japanese occupation.

Key Visitor Information (as of March 2026)

  • Location: Hillcrest Subdivision, Diversion Road, Matina Balusong, Davao City.
  • Operating Hours: The tunnel tour is open daily from 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM.
  • Entrance Fees: Adults: ₱70 Seniors: ₱60 Children: ₱40
  • Contact: For reservations or inquiries, you can reach them at +63 995 410 9839 or via their Official Facebook Page.

What to Expect

  • Guided Tour: Visitors are led through a 300-meter segment of the tunnel system. For safety, guests are required to wear helmets provided at the entrance.
  • Historical Artifacts: Inside, you'll find vintage weaponry (rifles, machine guns, explosives), original wooden tables, and shell casings.
  • Key Chambers: The tour showcases former sleeping quarters, a medical clinic, and dungeons used for prisoners.
  • Notable Replicas: The tunnel houses life-sized statues of Japanese soldiers and a replica of a Golden Buddha with gold bars, representing rumors of hidden Yamashita treasure.
  • Amenities: The site is now a full resort featuring a hotel, an outdoor swimming pool, and a restaurant serving Filipino, Japanese, and Western cuisine.
