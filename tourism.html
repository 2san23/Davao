Tourism in Davao del Sur

Davao del Sur is a province in Mindanao known for its diverse natural attractions, cultural heritage, and adventure destinations. From majestic mountains and waterfalls to pristine beaches and vibrant cultural sites, the province offers a unique travel experience for nature lovers, thrill-seekers, and cultural explorers alike.

Davao del Sur is one of the most diverse tourist destinations in Mindanao, offering a combination of eco-tourism, cultural heritage, adventure activities, and coastal relaxation. Its strategic location near Davao City makes it easily accessible, while still preserving its natural beauty and traditional way of life.

Philippine Eagle Center

The Philippine Eagle Center in Davao City is a conservation facility dedicated to protecting the critically endangered Philippine eagle.

Mount Apo

Mount Apo is the highest mountain and volcano in the Philippines.

Davao Crocodile Park

Davao Crocodile Park & Zoo is a wildlife conservation park and educational facility located in Riverfront Corporate City, Ma-a, Davao City.

Malagos garden Resort

Malagos Garden Resort is a 12-hectare agri-ecotourism destination in Davao City.

People's Park

People's Park in Davao City is a 4-hectare urban oasis known for its lush greenery, vibrant sculptures, and cultural significance.

D' Bone Collector Museum

D’ Bone Collector Museum in Davao City is a specialized natural history museum that showcases over 700 preserved animal skeletons from around the world.

Sm Lanag Premier

Four-story indoor shopping mall located along J.P. Laurel Avenue in Davao City, Philippines.

Japanese Tunnel

The Japanese Tunnel in Davao City

Roxas Avenue Night Market

The Roxas Avenue Night Market in Davao City is a popular daily open-air destination for street food, affordable shopping, and relaxation.
