Mount Apo

Mount Apo is the highest mountain and volcano in the Philippines, towering 2,954 meters (9,692 ft) above sea level on Mindanao island. As a protected Natural Park and a popular, challenging 2-4 day climb, it features a rugged landscape of volcanic craters, sulfur vents, and lush forests, home to the rare Philippine eagle.

Key Information About Mt. Apo:

  • Location: Bordering Davao City, Davao del Sur, and Cotabato
  • Status: Dormant stratovolcano (last eruption unknown, but has active sulfur vents).
  • Significance: Known as the "Grandfather of Philippine Mountains" and a sacred site to local Lumad and Bagobo tribes.
  • Hiking Info: Popular trails include Kapatagan, Kidapawan, and Sta. Cruz. Generally requires a 2-4 day hike with an expert guide.
  • Biodiversity: Home to over 200 bird species, including the Philippine Eagle.

Best Time to Visit:

The dry season, usually from March to May, is best to avoid slippery trails and for better visibility, say DENR official sources on Facebook.

Key Tips for Hikers:

  • Preparation: It is a challenging climb requiring good physical condition.
  • Guides: Hiring a local guide is crucial to avoid getting lost.
  • Gear: Bring adequate rain gear, windbreakers, and warm clothing, as the weather can change rapidly at high altitudes.
  • Regulations: It is a protected area, so permits from the Mt. Apo Foundation, Inc. are required.
