People's Park

People's Park in Davao City is a 4-hectare urban cultural-theme park known for its lush greenery, artistic sculptures, and the iconic durian-shaped dome. It is widely considered one of the cleanest and greenest public parks in the Philippines.

Visitor Information (as of March 2026)

  • Entrance Fee: Admission is free for everyone.
  • Operating Hours: The park typically follows a split schedule to accommodate the tropical climate: Morning Session: ~5:30 AM to 10:00 AM (often for joggers and fitness enthusiasts). Afternoon/Evening Session: 1:00 PM to 10:00 PM. Note: Specific times can vary slightly on weekends or for special events.
  • Location: J. Palma Gil St, Poblacion District, Davao City (near Apo View Hotel).

Key Attractions

  • Durian Dome: An iconic multi-level structure inspired by the durian fruit, housing an administrative office and cultural exhibits.
  • National Museum of the Philippines – Davao: Located within the park grounds, this newly opened museum features six stories of biodiversity, natural history, and Mindanaoan artifacts. Hours: Tuesday to Sunday, 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (Closed Mondays). Fee: Free admission.
  • The Promenade: A brick-laden 500-meter oval track used for jogging and walking.
  • Kublai Millan Sculptures: Large-scale artworks by the famous Mindanaoan artist, including the prominent Philippine Eagle replica.
  • Nature Features: Includes a mini-forest with over 1,100 plant species, a koi pond, and man-made waterfalls. Facebook

Park Guidelines & Facilities

  • Security: Visitors are subject to security inspections at the entrance. Prohibited items typically include weapons, cigarettes/lighters, and sometimes bottled water.
  • Smoking Prohibited: Davao City strictly enforces a no-smoking policy in public places, including this park.
  • Amenities: The park features a children’s playground, picnic tables, a public library, and accessible restrooms.
